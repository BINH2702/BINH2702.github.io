About

Research across changing distributions, tasks, and reasoning steps.

My goal is to build AI systems that continue learning after deployment while preserving what made them useful in the first place.

My earlier work studied personalized federated learning and domain generalization under decentralized data. I am now interested in how models can update efficiently, retain internal reasoning processes, and remain reliable when tasks, environments, or data distributions change.

I enjoy research that connects optimization, representation learning, and practical systems—especially when a method can be both theoretically motivated and experimentally testable.

Research areas
Continual learning, federated learning, domain generalization, latent reasoning

Research

Current directions

01

Continual learning & efficient adaptation

Methods that update models over a stream of tasks while controlling catastrophic forgetting, computation, and memory.

  • Low-rank adaptation
  • Replay
  • Forgetting diagnostics
02

Recursive & latent reasoning

Understanding how looped models preserve useful internal dynamics and reasoning procedures as their parameters change.

  • Looped models
  • Hidden states
  • Process retention
03

Federated domain generalization

Learning models that transfer to unseen domains when training data remain distributed across privacy-constrained clients.

  • Federated learning
  • Gradient matching
  • Distribution shift
04

Reliable embodied learning

Longer-term work on adaptation and safety for vision-language-action systems operating in changing environments.

  • Robot learning
  • VLA models
  • Safety
